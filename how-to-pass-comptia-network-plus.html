How to Pass CompTIA Network+

A realistic study plan for N10-009, what the exam actually tests, how long to study, which resources are worth your time, and the strategies that make a real difference on exam day.

üìä Network+ vs A+ ‚Äî what's actually different

A+ tests broad IT knowledge across hardware, OS, and basic networking. Network+ goes deep on networking specifically ‚Äî you need to understand how data actually moves across networks, how routing and switching decisions are made, and how to calculate subnets by hand. The troubleshooting scenarios are more complex and require systematic methodology rather than pattern-matching.

If you passed A+, you already have the foundation. Ports, protocols, the OSI model, and basic TCP/IP are review. The new material is subnetting, routing protocols, VLANs, WAN technologies, and network operations ‚Äî give these areas the most time.

The one topic that fails most candidates ‚Äî subnetting

Subnetting is the make-or-break topic on Network+. It's not optional, it can't be guessed, and it appears in multiple questions including performance-based questions where you have to calculate subnet ranges, usable hosts, and broadcast addresses under time pressure.

The good news is subnetting is completely learnable with the right approach. The bad news is most candidates underestimate how much practice it takes to do it quickly and accurately under exam conditions. Reading about subnetting is not enough ‚Äî you need to do hundreds of practice problems until the pattern is automatic.

‚ö° Subnetting study strategy

Learn the /24 to /32 range first ‚Äî these are the most commonly tested. Know the subnet mask, number of subnets, and usable hosts for each prefix length without having to calculate from scratch.

Practice daily, not in marathon sessions. 15‚Äì20 subnetting problems every day for 4 weeks builds fluency faster than doing 100 problems on one weekend. Speed comes from repetition, not understanding alone.

A free resource worth bookmarking: subnettingpractice.com ‚Äî unlimited randomised problems with instant feedback.

The study plan that works

1
Weeks 1‚Äì3 ‚Äî Networking fundamentals and subnetting
OSI model, IP addressing, subnetting ‚Äî build the foundation

Start with the OSI model and TCP/IP fundamentals ‚Äî even if you covered these for A+, review them with Network+ depth in mind. Then spend the majority of this phase on subnetting. Don't move on until you can calculate subnet ranges, usable hosts, and broadcast addresses consistently and quickly.

Review OSI model layers, protocols at each layer, and encapsulation
Learn IPv4 addressing ‚Äî classes, private ranges, CIDR notation
Master subnetting ‚Äî do at least 20 practice problems per day
Cover ports and protocols ‚Äî know the top 20 by number and purpose
2
Weeks 4‚Äì6 ‚Äî Network implementation and operations
Routing protocols, VLANs, switching, wireless, WAN

This phase covers the content that's most different from A+. Routing protocols, VLANs, and switching concepts require understanding how network decisions are made ‚Äî not just memorising names. Use diagrams and draw out network topologies as you study; visualising traffic flow accelerates understanding.

Routing protocols ‚Äî OSPF (link-state), RIP (distance vector), BGP (path vector)
VLANs ‚Äî purpose, trunking, inter-VLAN routing, 802.1Q
Switching ‚Äî STP, port security, MAC address tables
Wireless ‚Äî 802.11 standards, frequencies, WPA2 vs WPA3
WAN technologies ‚Äî MPLS, SD-WAN, broadband types
3

The security domain on Network+ is broader than many candidates expect ‚Äî not just firewalls and VPNs but also network hardening, attack types, and physical security. The troubleshooting domain requires internalising a systematic methodology: identify the problem, establish a theory, test the theory, implement the solution, verify, and document.

Firewalls, IDS/IPS, VPNs, DMZ architecture
Common network attacks ‚Äî DoS, MitM, ARP poisoning, DNS spoofing
Troubleshooting tools ‚Äî ping, tracert, nslookup, netstat, ipconfig
CompTIA's 7-step troubleshooting methodology ‚Äî memorise the order

Stop reading new material and switch entirely to practice exams. Take full 90-question timed tests under real conditions. For every wrong answer, understand not just what the right answer is but why ‚Äî Network+ scenarios often have two plausible answers and the reasoning matters more than the answer itself.

Take 3‚Äì4 full practice exams under timed conditions
Score each domain separately ‚Äî target anything below 75%
Continue daily subnetting practice throughout this phase
Book the exam when you're consistently hitting 80%+ on practice tests

‚ö° The most common reason people fail Network+

Underestimating subnetting. Candidates who can explain subnetting conceptually but can't calculate quickly under time pressure run out of time on PBQs and lose points they would have had. Subnetting speed comes only from repetitive practice ‚Äî there's no shortcut.

The second most common reason: skipping the troubleshooting methodology. CompTIA's 7-step process is directly tested and scenario questions specifically ask for the correct first, second, or next step. Memorise the order: identify the problem ‚Üí establish a theory ‚Üí test the theory ‚Üí establish a plan ‚Üí implement ‚Üí verify ‚Üí document.
